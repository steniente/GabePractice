Case Studies, Benchmark Data, & CX Research

Case Studies, Benchmark Data, & CX Research

The Health Museum

Project Goal

In late 2016 The Health Museum reached an all time low in patron attendance and public interest. THM knew they had to rebrand, rebuild, and get a clear picture of the market. THM wanted to know what the organization could do to revive and create the best patron experience. Our team conducted research to learn more about it's patrons and what they expected from the museum.

My role

As User Experience Developer, I helped organized a case study so our team could conducted a stakeholder and internal audit. During our meeting we used a priority matrix to understand the most important vs the least important User and Business goals.

Tools & Deliverables

After our SWOT (Strengths, Weakness, Oportunities, & Threats) analysis was concluded, we used an affinity diagram to curate a quick 6 minute survey with 20 questions. I set up 2 stationary kiosk ipads, and 2 more ipads for museum docents to survey patrons on our busiest day. (Free Family Thursday)


Raw Case Study Data

The challenge:

With so many types of people visiting the museum it was very difficult to understand the metrics of our primary customers. We narrowed our target audience to; mothers of young children, teachers, fieldtrips students, and medical students.

Solution: (How we met Sally?!)

When all the pieces came together, we finally met Sally! We also created personas and user stories for our other profiles.. We discovered The Health Museum needed more enaging and interesting topics. The top 4 topics were; The history of medical science, The body as art, The Texas Medical Center, and Popculture & Health. We knew it needed to be more interactive, fun, and have easy to understand high-level content like the nearby "Childrens Museum".

Results: (Got it done!)

The Health Museum listened and delivered 6 new exhilarating exhibits and had record breaking attendance over the next 2 years! In addition, the case study and deliverables were used to reformat the marketing website, online ticket sales platform, and social media presence. The museum was awarded an official partnership as a Smithsonian Affiliate. It was the first to recieve recognitive in the Houston area. I am so honered to be apart of that journey and help our surrounding communities.

crcu.org

CRCU June 2008 - January 2011

Project Goal

In june of 2008 CRCU had a website that looked straight from the 1990’s! Table design, HTML 4.0, and inline style sheets were deprecating. Adobe Flash was taking it's course on desktops but did'nt include the new mobile devices in the market. Our marketing team was tasked with creating a beautiful and easy to navigate website. Our goal was to target new memberships, increase loan revenue, and enage with our online audience.

My role

As a marketing specialist I collaborated with the senior vp of marketing and marketing director to revamp crcu.org. We worked closely with Digital Insight (An online banking platform) to implement a custom CMS and produce the website according to the latest web technolgy and brand standards.

The challenge:

How could we make our little credit union website look just as impresive like the big banks? Our team started with researching competitors website, profiling members data, wire-framing, mock-ups and hifi-prototyping.

Solution:

Apart from everything looking outdated, My knowledge of Gestalt theory, composition, and layout was challenge by overseeing the UI and UX development. We organized the information heavy content using the card sorting method so users can have a good user experience while quickly looking for products, services, calculators etc. For aesthetics, I used hyper realistic isolated imagery to create intuitive icons that resulted in quick and easy navigation.

Results:

Numerous full blown multi channel digital media campaigns (Email designs, social media, landing pages etc. ) drove record high analytics to the new redesigned crcu.org. With a new online user expereince, our Marketing team reached it's annual marketing goals and was able to surpase the highest CU assets in over 10 years. CRCU won 1st and 2nd place at the Credit Union National Association awards in marketing initiatives. Memberships, loans inquirys, and superceded annual marketing goals.
